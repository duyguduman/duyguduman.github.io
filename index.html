About Me

I am a neuroscientist with a background in Biomedical Engineering. My research focuses on EEG signal analysis, functional and effective connectivity, graph theory, and machine learning-based classification of brain network organization.

I am particularly interested in resting-state EEG, age-related changes in brain networks, and multimodal approaches that connect brain, body, and environment. My recent work also extends into environmental neuroscience, examining neurophysiological responses to natural and degraded landscapes.

02

Research Interests

EEG & Brain Connectivity

Functional and effective connectivity analyses including iCOH, PDC, and DTF.

Graph Theory

Complex brain network metrics such as global efficiency, modularity, clustering, path length, and nodal centrality.

Machine Learning

Classification of age-related brain network patterns using EEG-derived features.

Environmental Neuroscience

Investigation of neurophysiological responses to natural, urban, and degraded environments.
