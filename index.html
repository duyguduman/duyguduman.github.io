About

I am Duygu Duman, a neuroscientist with a background in Biomedical Engineering. My research focuses on EEG signal analysis, functional and effective connectivity, graph theoretical approaches, and machine learning-based classification of brain network organization.

My recent work investigates how aging affects brain network organization using resting-state EEG, connectivity analyses, and machine learning. I am also interested in environmental neuroscience, especially how natural, urban, and degraded landscapes affect stress, attention, and neurophysiological responses.

Research Interests

EEG & Brain Connectivity

Functional and effective connectivity analyses including iCOH, PDC, and DTF.

Graph Theory

Brain network metrics such as global efficiency, modularity, clustering, path length, and nodal centrality.

Machine Learning

Classification of age-related brain network patterns using EEG-derived features.

Environmental Neuroscience

Multimodal assessment of natural and degraded environments using EEG, posturography, autonomic and behavioral measures.
